## 2. Legal Basis

Under GDPR Article 17, data subjects have the right to obtain erasure of their personal data when:

- The data is no longer necessary for its original purpose
- The data subject withdraws consent (where consent was the lawful basis)
- The data subject objects to processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds

- Erasure is required for compliance with a legal obligation

**Response deadline:** 1 month from receipt of request (extendable by 2 months for complex requests).

## 7. Exceptions to Erasure (Art. 17(3))

The right to erasure does **not** apply when processing is necessary for:

| Exception | Example | Typical Retention |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| Freedom of expression and information | Published user reviews | Case-by-case |
| Legal obligation compliance | Tax records, financial regulations | 7-10 years |
| Public health reasons (Art. 9(2)(h)(i)) | Health data for public interest | Duration of need |
| Archiving in public interest | Research data, statistical purposes | Duration of study |
| Legal claims | Litigation hold, dispute evidence | Until resolution + limitation |

When an exception applies, document the specific legal basis and notify the data subject of the partial refusal with reasoning.
