## 1. Privacy Policy Generator

**File:** `src/generator/privacy-policy.ts`
**Rating: NEEDS WORK**

### GDPR Article 13 Compliance Check

Article 13 requires 12 specific information items (6 in para 1, 6 in para 2). Our template status:

| Clause | Requirement | Status |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Art. 13(1)(a) | Identity and contact details of controller + representative | PARTIAL -- only company name and email, no postal address, no EU representative |
| Art. 13(1)(b) | Contact details of Data Protection Officer | MISSING |
| Art. 13(1)(c) | Purposes + legal basis for each processing activity | PARTIAL -- purposes listed but legal basis only mentioned generically in "Your Rights" section, not per-purpose |
| Art. 13(1)(d) | Legitimate interests pursued (when relying on Art. 6(1)(f)) | MISSING as standalone disclosure |
| Art. 13(1)(e) | Recipients or categories of recipients | PARTIAL -- third-party services listed but no explicit "recipients" framing |
| Art. 13(1)(f) | International transfers + safeguards (adequacy decisions, SCCs) | MISSING |
| Art. 13(2)(a) | Retention periods or criteria | PRESENT |
| Art. 13(2)(b) | Data subject rights (access, rectify, erase, restrict, port, object) | PRESENT |
| Art. 13(2)(c) | Right to withdraw consent | MISSING as explicit right |
| Art. 13(2)(d) | Right to lodge complaint with supervisory authority | PRESENT |
| Art. 13(2)(e) | Whether data provision is statutory/contractual + consequences of not providing | MISSING |
| Art. 13(2)(f) | Automated decision-making/profiling details | MISSING |

### Missing Clauses with Example Text

**1. DPO Contact (Art. 13(1)(b)) -- HIGH PRIORITY**
```
## Data Protection Officer

Our Data Protection Officer can be contacted at:
- **Name:** [DPO Name]
- **Email:** [dpo@example.com]

If we are not required to appoint a DPO, our primary data protection contact is listed above.
```

**2. International Data Transfers (Art. 13(1)(f)) -- HIGH PRIORITY**
```
## International Data Transfers

Your personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside the
European Economic Area (EEA). Where such transfers occur, we ensure appropriate
safeguards are in place, including:

- Transfers to countries with an EU adequacy decision
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
- EU-US Data Privacy Framework certification (where applicable)

You may request a copy of the applicable safeguards by contacting us.
```

**3. Legal Basis Per Purpose (Art. 13(1)(c)/(d)) -- HIGH PRIORITY**
The current template mentions legal basis generically in the rights section. GDPR requires a purpose-by-legal-basis mapping. Example:
```
| Purpose | Legal Basis | Details |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Providing the service | Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) | Necessary to fulfill our agreement |
| Analytics | Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) | Only with your opt-in consent |
| Security monitoring | Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) | Protecting our service and users |
| Payment processing | Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)) | Tax and financial regulations |
```

**4. Right to Withdraw Consent (Art. 13(2)(c)) -- MEDIUM PRIORITY**
```
Where we process your data based on consent, you have the right to withdraw
that consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of
processing carried out before withdrawal. You can withdraw consent by
[describe method, e.g., adjusting your cookie preferences, emailing us].
```

**5. Whether Data Provision Is Required (Art. 13(2)(e)) -- MEDIUM PRIORITY**
```
## Necessity of Data Provision

Some personal data is required to provide you with our services (e.g., account
registration). Where data provision is mandatory, we will indicate this at the
point of collection. Failure to provide required data may result in our
inability to provide you with the service.
```

**6. Automated Decision-Making (Art. 13(2)(f)) -- MEDIUM PRIORITY (especially when AI services detected)**
```
## Automated Decision-Making

[If applicable:] We use automated processing, including profiling, in the
following ways: [describe]. You have the right not to be subject to decisions
based solely on automated processing that produce legal or similarly
significant effects. You may request human intervention, express your point
of view, or contest such decisions.

[If not applicable:] We do not currently use automated decision-making or
profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you.
```

### Comparison with Industry Standards (Stripe, Linear)

Stripe's privacy policy includes jurisdiction-specific sections (GDPR, LGPD, FADP, CCPA), a separate cookie policy reference, explicit data sharing categories by recipient type, and a Data Privacy Framework policy. Our template lacks:
- Jurisdiction-specific conditional sections beyond EU/California
- Explicit data sharing categories (internal vs. external recipients)
- Reference to Data Privacy Framework or adequacy mechanisms

## 3. AI Disclosure Generator

**File:** `src/generator/ai-disclosure.ts`
**Rating: NEEDS WORK**

### EU AI Act Article 50 Compliance Check

| Requirement | Status | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Art. 50(1): Inform users of AI interaction | PRESENT | Covered in Section 4.1 and user rights |
| Art. 50(2): Synthetic content marking (machine-readable) | MISSING | Template does not address watermarking or machine-readable marking of AI outputs |
| Art. 50(3): Emotion recognition/biometric disclosure | MISSING | No conditional section for these systems |
| Art. 50(4): Deepfake disclosure | MISSING | No conditional section for synthetic media |
| Art. 50(5): Accessible, clear disclosure at first interaction | PARTIAL | Document exists but no guidance on how/when to surface it to users |
| Risk classification self-assessment | PARTIAL | Categories listed but no guidance on how to actually classify |

### Critical Missing Items

**1. Synthetic Content Machine-Readable Marking (Art. 50(2)) -- HIGH PRIORITY**

The EU AI Act requires that AI-generated content be marked in a machine-readable format (watermarking, metadata). Our template entirely omits this. This is significant because the Code of Practice on marking and labelling AI-generated content (draft published Dec 2025, final expected June 2026) establishes technical standards.

Example text:
```
## Synthetic Content Marking

Where this application generates synthetic text, image, audio, or video
content using AI, such outputs are marked as AI-generated using
machine-readable metadata in accordance with Article 50(2) of the EU AI Act.

**Technical implementation:**
- [Describe marking method, e.g., C2PA metadata, watermarking, or embedded flags]
- Marking is applied at the point of generation and is designed to be
  interoperable with detection tools

> **Action required:** Implement a technical mechanism to mark AI-generated
> outputs. Consult the EU Code of Practice on AI-generated content labelling
> for guidance on compliant marking methods.
```

**2. Emotion Recognition / Biometric Categorization (Art. 50(3)) -- MEDIUM PRIORITY**

Should be conditionally generated if relevant services are detected:
```
## Emotion Recognition and Biometric Systems

[If applicable:] This application uses AI-based emotion recognition /
biometric categorization. In accordance with Article 50(3) of the EU AI Act,
we inform you that [describe system and its operation]. Personal data
processed through this system is handled in accordance with the GDPR.

You have the right to be informed when exposed to such systems and to
exercise your data protection rights as described in our Privacy Policy.
```

**3. Deepfake / Synthetic Media Disclosure (Art. 50(4)) -- MEDIUM PRIORITY**

Conditional section for image/video/audio generation:
```
## Synthetic Media Disclosure

This application can generate or manipulate [image/audio/video] content
using AI. In accordance with Article 50(4) of the EU AI Act, we disclose
that such content has been artificially generated or manipulated.

All synthetic media produced by this application is labeled as AI-generated
at the point of output.
```

**4. First-Interaction Disclosure Guidance (Art. 50(5)) -- MEDIUM PRIORITY**
```
## How to Display This Disclosure

Under Article 50(5), users must be informed at the point of first
interaction with the AI system, in a clear and distinguishable manner,
meeting accessibility standards (Directive (EU) 2019/882).

**Recommended implementation:**
- Display a notice before the user first interacts with AI features
- Include an AI indicator icon or badge on AI-generated content
- Link to this full disclosure from the in-app notification
```

## 4. Cookie Policy Generator

**Rating: NEEDS WORK**

### ePrivacy Directive and GDPR Cookie Requirements Check

| Requirement | Status | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Cookie types and purposes | PRESENT | Essential and analytics cookies listed |
| Third-party cookie identification | PRESENT | Listed with data collected |
| Cookie duration | PRESENT | In table format |
| Consent mechanism description | MISSING | No description of how consent is obtained |
| Consent withdrawal method | MISSING | Only mentions browser settings, no app-level mechanism |
| Prior consent blocking (non-essential cookies) | MISSING | No mention that cookies are blocked until consent |
| Granular category-level consent | MISSING | No description of category choices |
| Consent logging/records | MISSING | No mention of storing consent records |
| Legal basis for cookies | MISSING | No mention of ePrivacy Directive Art. 5(3) or GDPR Art. 6(1)(a) |
| Functional cookies category | MISSING | Only essential and analytics, no "functional" or "marketing" categories |
| Link to third-party cookie policies | PARTIAL | Opt-out links present but not privacy policy links |

### Example Text for Missing Items

**1. Consent Mechanism (ePrivacy Art. 5(3) + GDPR) -- HIGH PRIORITY**
```
## Your Consent

In accordance with Article 5(3) of the ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC) and
the GDPR, we obtain your consent before placing non-essential cookies on
your device.

**How we obtain consent:**
- A cookie consent banner is displayed on your first visit
- Non-essential cookies are blocked until you provide consent
- You can accept all, reject all, or customize your preferences by category
- Accept and reject options are presented with equal prominence

**Cookie categories requiring consent:**
- Analytics cookies
- Marketing/advertising cookies
- Functional cookies (non-essential)

**Consent records:** We log your consent decision, including the date, time,
and categories selected, as required by the GDPR's accountability principle.
```

**2. Consent Withdrawal -- MEDIUM PRIORITY**
```
## Withdrawing Consent

You can withdraw or change your cookie consent at any time by:
- Clicking the "Cookie Preferences" link in our website footer
- [Other method]

Withdrawal of consent is as easy as giving consent. Upon withdrawal, we
will cease using the relevant cookies, though some data already collected
may be retained as described in our Privacy Policy.
```

**3. Legal Basis -- MEDIUM PRIORITY**
```
## Legal Basis

- **Strictly necessary cookies:** Exempt from consent under ePrivacy
  Directive Art. 5(3) as they are essential for the service you requested
- **All other cookies:** Placed only with your prior consent under GDPR
  Art. 6(1)(a) and ePrivacy Directive Art. 5(3)
```

## 5. Data Processing Agreement Generator

**File:** `src/generator/dpa.ts`
**Rating: GOOD**

### GDPR Article 28(3) Compliance Check

All 8 mandatory clauses are covered:

| Art. 28(3) Clause | Requirement | Status |
|-------------------|------------|--------|
| (a) | Process only on documented instructions | PRESENT (Section 5.1) |
| (b) | Confidentiality obligations | PRESENT (Section 5.2) |
| (c) | Security measures per Art. 32 | PRESENT (Section 5.3, Section 8) |
| (d) | Sub-processor conditions | PRESENT (Section 5.4, Section 6) |
| (e) | Assist with data subject rights | PRESENT (Section 5.5) |
| (f) | Assist with Art. 32-36 obligations | PRESENT (Section 5.6) |
| (g) | Delete/return data on termination | PRESENT (Section 5.7, Section 10) |
| (h) | Audit and inspection rights | PRESENT (Section 5.8, Section 11) |

### Minor Improvements Needed

| Item | Issue | Priority |
|------|-------|----------|
| DPIA mention | No explicit mention of Data Protection Impact Assessment assistance (Art. 35-36) | LOW |
| DPO reference | No placeholder for DPO contact in the DPA itself | LOW |
| Liability and indemnification | No clause on processor liability for breaches | MEDIUM |
| SCCs annex | Template mentions SCCs but does not include or reference the annex | LOW |
| Specific retention periods | Retention section is generic; should prompt user for specific periods per processor | LOW |

Example for liability clause:
```
## Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations and
exclusions of liability set out in the underlying service agreement. The
Processor shall be liable for damage caused by processing that infringes
the GDPR, in accordance with Article 82 of the GDPR. The Processor shall
be exempt from liability if it proves that it is not in any way responsible
for the event giving rise to the damage.
```

## Priority Ranking -- What to Fix First

### P0 -- Critical (legal compliance gaps)
1. **Privacy Policy: International data transfers clause** (Art. 13(1)(f)) -- Required for any app using US-based services (Stripe, OpenAI, etc.)
2. **Privacy Policy: Legal basis per processing purpose** (Art. 13(1)(c)/(d)) -- GDPR fundamentally requires this mapping
3. **Privacy Policy: DPO / controller contact details** (Art. 13(1)(a)/(b)) -- Must include postal address, representative
4. **AI Disclosure: Synthetic content machine-readable marking** (Art. 50(2)) -- Core EU AI Act obligation effective Aug 2026

### P1 -- High (functional gaps)
5. **Terms of Service: Indemnification clause** -- Standard in all SaaS ToS
6. **Terms of Service: Dispute resolution / arbitration** -- Without this, jurisdiction is ambiguous
7. **Terms of Service: Privacy Policy cross-reference** -- Required link between documents
8. **Cookie Policy: Consent mechanism description** -- ePrivacy Directive core requirement
9. **Privacy Policy: Right to withdraw consent** (Art. 13(2)(c))
10. **Privacy Policy: Automated decision-making disclosure** (Art. 13(2)(f))

### P2 -- Medium (completeness improvements)
11. **Cookie Policy: Legal basis for cookies** (ePrivacy + GDPR)
12. **Cookie Policy: Consent withdrawal mechanism**
13. **Terms of Service: Severability, entire agreement, assignment**
14. **AI Disclosure: First-interaction disclosure guidance** (Art. 50(5))
15. **AI Disclosure: Emotion recognition conditional section** (Art. 50(3))
16. **DPA: Liability clause** (Art. 82 GDPR)
17. **Privacy Policy: Data provision necessity** (Art. 13(2)(e))

### P3 -- Low (nice to have)
18. **Terms of Service: Force majeure, export compliance**
19. **DPA: DPIA assistance reference**
20. **DPA: SCC annex template**
21. **AI Disclosure: Deepfake disclosure conditional section** (Art. 50(4))
