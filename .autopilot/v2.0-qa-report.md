## 2. GDPR Article 13 Compliance Audit

Generated privacy policy for a comprehensive test project with: OpenAI, Stripe, Supabase Auth, PostHog, Sentry, and Resend.

| Clause | Requirement | Status | Notes |
|--------|------------|--------|-------|
| Art. 13(1)(a) | Controller identity + contact | PARTIAL | Company name "TestCo GmbH" present in intro (section 1). Contact email in section 7. **Missing:** No registered address, no company registration number. |
| Art. 13(1)(b) | DPO contact details | FAIL | Config accepts `dpoName` and `dpoEmail` fields, but the privacy policy generator **completely ignores them**. "Max Mueller" and "dpo@testco.eu" do not appear anywhere in the output. |
| Art. 13(1)(c) | Purposes + legal basis per purpose | PARTIAL | Legal bases listed in section 6 (consent, contract, legitimate interest) but **not mapped to specific processing purposes**. GDPR requires legal basis stated per purpose, not as a generic list. |
| Art. 13(1)(d) | Legitimate interests (if applicable) | PARTIAL | "Security and fraud prevention" mentioned as legitimate interest, but no detail on balancing test or specific interests pursued. Minimally acceptable. |
| Art. 13(1)(e) | Recipients / categories of recipients | PASS | Third-party services listed with categories and data collected (section 3). Sufficient for Art. 13(1)(e). |
| Art. 13(1)(f) | International transfers + safeguards | FAIL | **No mention whatsoever** of international data transfers. OpenAI, Stripe, PostHog, and Sentry are US-based. Must disclose transfers to third countries and safeguards (SCCs, adequacy decisions). This is a critical GDPR gap. |
| Art. 13(2)(a) | Retention periods | PASS | Section 5 provides retention periods for account data, usage data, and payment data. Could be more specific but meets minimum requirement. |
| Art. 13(2)(b) | Data subject rights | PASS | Section 6 lists access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection, restriction rights. Covers GDPR requirements. |
| Art. 13(2)(c) | Right to withdraw consent | FAIL | **No mention** of the right to withdraw consent at any time, nor how to exercise it. Section 6 mentions consent as a legal basis but does not state it can be withdrawn. |
| Art. 13(2)(d) | Right to lodge complaint with supervisory authority | PASS | Section 6 states "You have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority." Present. |
| Art. 13(2)(e) | Whether data provision is statutory/contractual requirement | FAIL | **No mention** of whether providing personal data is a statutory or contractual requirement, or necessary to enter a contract, and consequences of not providing data. |
| Art. 13(2)(f) | Automated decision-making / profiling | FAIL | **No mention** of automated decision-making or profiling. With AI services (OpenAI) detected, this is especially relevant. Even if no automated decisions are made, Art. 13(2)(f) should be addressed. |

### Summary: 4 PASS, 3 PARTIAL, 5 FAIL

**Critical gaps (FAIL):**
1. DPO contact details not rendered despite being in config
2. International data transfers clause entirely missing
3. Right to withdraw consent not mentioned
4. Data provision requirements not addressed
5. Automated decision-making / profiling not addressed

### Additional Issues Found

1. **Duplicate Sentry detection:** `@sentry/nextjs` is detected, but `@sentry/node` and `@sentry/react` also appear as separate services (from shared `SENTRY_DSN` env var). This is a pre-existing scanner bug (BUG-8 from v1.0), not a v2.0 issue.

2. **Section numbering fragility:** The numbering in `privacy-policy.ts` uses ternary expressions (`aiServices.length > 0 ? "5" : "4"`) rather than an auto-incrementing counter. If more conditional sections are added (e.g., DPO, transfers), this will break. Already flagged in v1.0 PM notes.

3. **Config fields accepted but unused in privacy policy:**
   - `dpoName` -- accepted in config, passed to GeneratorContext, but never read by `generatePrivacyPolicy()`
   - `dpoEmail` -- same
   - `jurisdiction` -- same
   - `euRepresentative` -- same
   - `dataRetentionDays` -- same (hardcoded "26 months" instead)
