# CCPA/CPRA Privacy Policy Requirements

**California Consumer Privacy Act (Cal. Civ. Code 1798.100 et seq.)**
**As amended by California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)**

Sources: [California Legislature](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displayText.xhtml?division=3.&part=4.&lawCode=CIV&title=1.81.5) | [CPRA Resource Center](https://www.caprivacy.org/cpra-text/)

---

## Section 1798.100 - General Duties / Notice at Collection

- (a) A consumer shall have the right to request that a business that collects a consumer's personal information disclose to that consumer the categories and specific pieces of personal information the business has collected.
- (b) A business that controls the collection of a consumer's personal information shall, at or before the point of collection, inform consumers as to:
  1. The categories of personal information to be collected
  2. The purposes for which the categories of personal information are collected or used
  3. Whether that information is sold or shared
  4. The length of time the business intends to retain each category of personal information (or criteria for determining that period)
- A business shall not collect additional categories of personal information or use personal information collected for additional purposes without providing notice consistent with this section.

## Section 1798.105 - Right to Deletion

- A consumer has the right to request deletion of personal information collected from the consumer.
- The privacy policy must disclose this right.

## Section 1798.106 - Right to Correction

- A consumer has the right to request that a business correct inaccurate personal information.

## Section 1798.110 - Right to Know (Disclosure)

- Privacy policy must disclose:
  1. Categories of personal information collected
  2. Categories of sources from which information is collected
  3. Business or commercial purpose for collecting, selling, or sharing
  4. Categories of third parties to whom information is disclosed
  5. Specific pieces of personal information collected about the consumer (upon request)

## Section 1798.115 - Sale/Sharing Disclosure

- Privacy policy must disclose:
  1. Categories of personal information sold or shared in the preceding 12 months
  2. Categories of third parties to whom information was sold or shared
  3. Categories of personal information disclosed for a business purpose in the preceding 12 months
  4. If no sale or sharing occurred, a statement to that effect

## Section 1798.120 - Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing

- Consumers have the right to direct a business that sells or shares personal information to stop selling or sharing.
- Business must provide notice that information may be sold or shared and that consumers have the right to opt out.

## Section 1798.121 - Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information

- Consumers may limit the use and disclosure of sensitive personal information to that use which is necessary.

## Section 1798.125 - Non-Discrimination

- A business shall not discriminate against a consumer because the consumer exercised any rights under the CCPA.
- This right must be disclosed in the privacy policy.

## Section 1798.130 - Business Compliance Requirements

- Make available to consumers two or more designated methods for submitting requests (including at minimum a toll-free telephone number and, if the business has a website, a web address)
- Disclose and deliver required information free of charge within 45 days
- Update privacy policy disclosures at least once every 12 months
- Include in the privacy policy a description of consumer rights and methods for submitting requests

## Section 1798.135 - Opt-Out Link Requirements

- Provide a clear and conspicuous link on the business's internet homepage titled "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information"
- Provide a link titled "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" (if applicable)
- Alternatively, use a single clearly labeled link that enables opt-out of both sale/sharing and limiting sensitive information
- Honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal as a valid opt-out request
