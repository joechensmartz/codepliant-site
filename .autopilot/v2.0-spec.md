## Objective

Rewrite the privacy policy generator (`src/generator/privacy-policy.ts`) to include all 12 GDPR Article 13 required information items. The v1.0 legal audit found 6 missing or incomplete clauses. This spec covers every change needed: new template sections, config schema updates, lawful basis detection logic, data retention mapping, international transfer detection, and section numbering refactor.

---

## 1. Missing GDPR Art. 13 Clauses — Exact Sections to Add

The legal audit (`.autopilot/v1.0-legal-audit.md`) identified these 6 gaps. All must be addressed.

### 1.1 DPO Contact Details (Art. 13(1)(b)) — P0

Add a new section immediately after the Introduction. Rendered only when `dpoEmail` or `dpoName` is configured; otherwise render a fallback pointing to the general contact.

**Exact template text:**

```markdown
## {n}. Data Protection Officer

Our Data Protection Officer can be contacted at:

- **Name:** {dpoName}
- **Email:** {dpoEmail}

If you have concerns about how we handle your personal data, you may contact our DPO directly.
```

**Fallback (no DPO configured):**

```markdown
## {n}. Data Protection Contact

Our primary contact for data protection matters is:

- **Email:** {contactEmail}

If your organization is required to appoint a Data Protection Officer under GDPR Article 37, update your `.codepliantrc.json` with `dpoName` and `dpoEmail`.
```

**Implementation notes:**
- Read `dpoName` and `dpoEmail` from `GeneratorContext` (new fields, see Section 6 below).
- If both are empty/undefined, use the fallback variant.

### 1.2 Lawful Basis Per Processing Purpose (Art. 13(1)(c) + 13(1)(d)) — P0

Replace the generic legal basis mention currently buried in the "Your Rights > GDPR" subsection. Add a dedicated section with a purpose-to-legal-basis table.

**Exact template text:**

```markdown
## {n}. Legal Basis for Processing

Under GDPR Article 6, we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

| Purpose | Legal Basis | Details |
|---------|------------|---------|
{rows}

Where we rely on **legitimate interest** (Art. 6(1)(f)), our interests are: ensuring service security, preventing fraud, and improving service quality. You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interest.
```

**Row generation logic** (see Section 2 for the full mapping):

Each detected service category maps to a purpose and legal basis. The generator iterates over the detected service categories (from `scan.services`) and produces one row per unique purpose.

### 1.3 International Data Transfers (Art. 13(1)(f)) — P0

Add a section that auto-detects whether any US-based services are present in the scan results.

**Exact template text (when US-based services detected):**

```markdown
## {n}. International Data Transfers

Your personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside the European Economic Area (EEA), including the United States. The following services operate from or transfer data to the US:

{list of US-based service names}

Where such transfers occur, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

- EU-US Data Privacy Framework certification (where applicable)
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
- Transfers to countries with an EU adequacy decision

You may request a copy of the applicable transfer safeguards by contacting us.
```

**Fallback (no international transfers detected):**

```markdown
## {n}. International Data Transfers

Based on our automated analysis, all detected data processing services operate within the EEA. If your deployment uses additional services that transfer data internationally, update this section accordingly.
```

**US-based service detection** (see Section 3 for the full list).

### 1.4 Right to Withdraw Consent (Art. 13(2)(c)) — P1

Add to the existing "Your Rights" section, inside the GDPR subsection, as a new bullet point and explanatory paragraph.

**Exact text to add within the GDPR rights subsection:**

```markdown
- **Withdraw Consent:** Where we process your data based on consent, withdraw that consent at any time

Where we process your data based on consent (see Legal Basis section above), you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal. You can withdraw consent by contacting us at {contactEmail} or by adjusting your preferences in the application settings.
```

### 1.5 Whether Data Provision Is Required (Art. 13(2)(e)) — P1

Add as a new section after Data Retention.

**Exact template text:**

```markdown
## {n}. Necessity of Data Provision

Some personal data is required to provide you with our services (e.g., account registration, payment processing). Where data provision is mandatory, we indicate this at the point of collection. Failure to provide required data may result in our inability to deliver the requested service.

Optional data collection (such as analytics) is clearly identified and requires your consent.
```

### 1.6 Automated Decision-Making (Art. 13(2)(f)) — P1

Conditionally rendered based on whether AI services are detected.

**When AI services are detected:**

```markdown
## {n}. Automated Decision-Making

This application uses automated processing through AI services ({aiServiceNames}). In accordance with GDPR Article 22:

- We inform you of any automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects
- You have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing
- You may request human intervention, express your point of view, or contest automated decisions

If automated decision-making is used beyond content generation (e.g., for eligibility, scoring, or profiling), you will be informed separately at the point of use.
```

**When no AI services detected:**

```markdown
## {n}. Automated Decision-Making

We do not currently use automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you as defined under GDPR Article 22.
```

## 2. Lawful Basis Detection — Service Category to Legal Basis Mapping

Builder should implement a `getLawfulBasisMapping` function (or equivalent inline logic) in `privacy-policy.ts` that maps each `ServiceCategory` to a purpose row. The mapping:

| Service Category | Purpose Text | Legal Basis | GDPR Article | Details Text |
|-----------------|-------------|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| `auth` | Providing user authentication | Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) | 6(1)(b) | Necessary to verify identity and maintain your account |
| `payment` | Processing payments | Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) | 6(1)(b) | Necessary to fulfill purchase transactions |
| `payment` | Tax and financial compliance | Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)) | 6(1)(c) | Required by tax and financial regulations |
| `database` | Storing user account data | Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) | 6(1)(b) | Necessary to provide the service you requested |
| `analytics` | Understanding service usage | Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) | 6(1)(a) | Only with your opt-in consent |
| `advertising` | Displaying relevant advertisements | Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) | 6(1)(a) | Only with your opt-in consent |
| `ai` | Providing AI-powered features | Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) | 6(1)(a) | Only with your opt-in consent; see AI section |
| `email` | Sending transactional emails | Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) | 6(1)(b) | Necessary to communicate about your account |
| `monitoring` | Detecting and fixing errors | Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) | 6(1)(f) | Ensuring service stability and security |
| `storage` | Storing user-uploaded content | Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) | 6(1)(b) | Necessary to provide the file storage you requested |
| `social` | Social login and sharing | Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) | 6(1)(a) | Only when you choose to connect a social account |
| `other` | Supporting service functionality | Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) | 6(1)(f) | Improving service quality and user experience |

**Implementation:**
- Only include rows for categories actually detected in `scan.services`.
- Deduplicate by purpose (e.g., if both `stripe` and `@paypal/checkout-server-sdk` are detected, only one "Processing payments" row).
- `payment` generates two rows (contract + legal obligation). All others generate one row each.

## 4. Data Retention Recommendations Per Service Type

Replace the current hardcoded retention section with a dynamic table based on detected service categories.

**Retention mapping:**

| Service Category | Data Type | Recommended Retention | Rationale |
|-----------------|-----------|----------------------|-----------|
| `auth` | Account credentials and session data | Until account deletion + 30 days | Allow recovery window |
| `payment` | Transaction records and billing data | 7 years | Tax/legal compliance (varies by jurisdiction) |
| `analytics` | Usage and behavioral data | 26 months (Google Analytics default) or `dataRetentionDays` from config | Industry standard; configurable |
| `ai` | User prompts and AI outputs | 90 days or `dataRetentionDays` from config | Minimize retained conversational data |
| `email` | Email addresses and content | Until unsubscribe + 30 days | GDPR purpose limitation |
| `monitoring` | Error reports and logs | 90 days | Standard log retention |
| `storage` | User-uploaded files | Until user deletes or account deletion | User controls their content |
| `database` | Persistent user data | Until account deletion | Core service data |
| `advertising` | Ad interaction data | 13 months | ePrivacy cookie maximum |
| `social` | Social profile data | Until disconnect or account deletion | User controls social links |

**Exact template text:**

```markdown
## {n}. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law.

| Data Type | Retention Period | Reason |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
{rows}

When data is no longer needed, it is securely deleted or anonymized. You may request earlier deletion by exercising your erasure right (see Your Rights section).
```

**Implementation:**
- Only include rows for service categories actually detected.
- If `dataRetentionDays` is set in config, use it as the override for analytics/AI rows and note "(configured)" in the Reason column.

## 5. Section Numbering Refactor

**Current problem:** Section numbers are hardcoded as `"2"`, `"3"`, etc., and some use ternary expressions like `aiServices.length > 0 ? "5" : "4"`. This breaks when new sections are inserted.

**Solution:** Use an auto-incrementing counter.

```typescript
let sectionNumber = 0;
function nextSection(): number {
  return ++sectionNumber;
}
```

Then every section heading uses `## ${nextSection()}. Section Title` instead of a hardcoded number.

**Final section order (all sections, some conditional):**

1. Introduction (always)
2. Data Protection Officer / Data Protection Contact (always; variant depends on config)
3. Information We Collect (always)
4. Third-Party Services (conditional: non-database services detected)
5. Artificial Intelligence (conditional: AI services detected)
6. Legal Basis for Processing (always when services detected)
7. International Data Transfers (always when services detected)
8. Data Retention (always)
9. Necessity of Data Provision (always)
10. Your Rights (always; GDPR subsection includes withdraw consent)
11. Automated Decision-Making (always; variant depends on AI detection)
12. Contact (always)

Conditional sections are skipped entirely (not rendered with empty content), and the counter only increments for rendered sections.

## 9. Acceptance Criteria

1. Generated privacy policy contains all 12 GDPR Art. 13 clauses (6 existing + 6 new).
2. Section numbers auto-increment correctly regardless of which conditional sections are rendered.
3. When DPO config is provided, the DPO section shows the configured name/email. When absent, fallback text renders.
4. Legal basis table only includes rows for detected service categories.
5. International transfers section lists detected US-based services by name.
6. Data retention table only includes rows for detected service categories and respects `dataRetentionDays` override.
7. Automated decision-making section toggles variant based on AI service detection.
8. `tsc` compiles without errors.
9. All existing tests pass (update test expectations for the new template structure).
10. QA: compare a generated policy (for a project using Stripe + NextAuth + Sentry + OpenAI) against Stripe's real privacy policy and verify all 12 Art. 13 items are present.
